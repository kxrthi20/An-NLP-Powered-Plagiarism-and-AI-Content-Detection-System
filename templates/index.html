What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the representation of another author's language, thoughts, ideas, or expressions as one's own original work. In academic and professional contexts, it is considered a serious breach of ethics and intellectual property rights.

Why are we using a Plagiarism Detector?

With the vast amount of digital information readily accessible, it has become incredibly easy to copy content. A high-quality plagiarism detection system helps educators, authors, and researchers verify originality, protect copyright, and instantly process large volumes of assignments without manual, error-prone reading.

What are the Levels of Plagiarism?

  • High Plagiarism (>80%): Substantial verbatim copying. Indicates direct copy-pasting with little to no modification or original thought.
  • Medium Plagiarism (40% - 80%): Significant structural or phrasing similarities. Indicates heavy paraphrasing and shared vocabulary. Requires careful citation checking.
  • Low Plagiarism (<40%): Standard generic phrasing overlap or identical foundational terminology. Generaly indicates completely original structural work.
